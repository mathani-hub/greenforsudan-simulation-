About YCNS: Why Negotiation Matters

The Challenge: Closing the Policy Gap

We address Sudan’s escalating climate risks—from catastrophic drought, floods, conflict—and the urgent gap in youth representation in policy-shaping. The decisions made today determine the stability of tomorrow.

The Methodology: Experiential Learning

"We turn theory into practical, high-stakes decision-making. Instead of reading about the UN, you become the negotiator."

The Outcome: A National Pipeline

The program builds a national pipeline of informed leaders who can translate global policy into local resilience and environmental peace.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What if I miss a session?

Attendance is encouraged, but not mandatory. Archived documents and pre-reading materials are available to help you catch up on the context for the following session.

What are the technical requirements?

You need a stable internet connection and access to a virtual meeting platform (Zoom/Google Meet). A laptop or tablet is recommended, though a mobile device can work for attendance.

Do I need a university degree?

Absolutely not. The program is open to all Sudanese youth (16-35). We value diverse perspectives over formal qualifications.
